T-90M PRORYV

The T‑90 is a Russian main battle tank introduced in 1992, weighing about 46–48 tonnes with a crew of three. It uses a 125mm smoothbore gun that can fire shells and guided missiles, backed by machine guns, and is powered by a 1,000‑hp diesel engine reaching 60 km/h with a 550 km range. Protected by composite and reactive armor, it is compact, efficient, widely exported, and continuously upgraded, its unit cost was about $6-7 million.

T-80

The T-80 is a third-generation Soviet main battle tank, first introduced in 1976, and notable as the world’s first production tank to use a gas turbine engine. Designed for speed and breakthrough operations, it combines high mobility with strong firepower and protection. Armed with a 125mm smoothbore gun equipped with an autoloader, the T-80 can fire a variety of ammunition types, including anti-tank guided missiles, giving it versatility against both armored and fortified targets. Its composite armor, reinforced with explosive reactive armor in later variants, provides solid defense against modern threats. The T-80’s turbine engine delivers excellent acceleration and performance in cold climates, making it highly effective in diverse operational environments. Estimated unit cost: $2.2–3 million USD

BMPT-TERMINATOR

The BMPT “Terminator” is a heavily armed armored fighting vehicle developed by Russia to provide direct fire support for tanks and infantry in high-intensity combat. Built on the chassis of the T-72 main battle tank, it combines strong protection with formidable firepower, making it highly effective in urban warfare and complex battlefield environments. The Terminator is equipped with twin 30mm 2A42 autocannons, four Ataka-T anti-tank guided missile launchers, two AGS-17 or AGS-30 automatic grenade launchers, and a coaxial 7.62mm machine gun, allowing it to engage a wide range of targets including enemy armor, fortifications, and infantry. Its advanced fire control systems and multi-crew configuration ensure simultaneous engagement of multiple threats, while its heavy armor provides resilience against modern anti-tank weapons. Estimated unit cost: $3.5–4 million USD

BMP-3

The BMP-3 is a modern Russian infantry fighting vehicle designed to deliver a powerful combination of mobility, protection, and firepower for mechanized units. Built with aluminum alloy armor reinforced by steel plates, it provides effective defense against small-arms fire and artillery fragments while maintaining agility on the battlefield. The BMP-3 is armed with a 100mm rifled gun capable of firing both shells and guided missiles, a 30mm autocannon, and three 7.62mm machine guns, giving it exceptional versatility against infantry, fortifications, and armored targets. Its amphibious capability allows rapid river crossings and operations in diverse terrains, while advanced optics and fire control systems enhance accuracy and situational awareness. Compact yet heavily armed, the BMP-3 is regarded as one of the most formidable IFVs in service today. Estimated unit cost: $3.4–3.6 million USD

BMP-2

The BMP-2 is a Soviet-designed infantry fighting vehicle introduced in the early 1980s, built to enhance mobility and firepower for mechanized infantry units. Compact yet versatile, it is armed with a 30mm 2A42 autocannon, a coaxial 7.62mm machine gun, and an AT-5 Spandrel anti-tank guided missile launcher, giving it the ability to engage both infantry and armored targets effectively. Its welded steel armor provides protection against small-arms fire and artillery fragments, while its amphibious capability allows rapid river crossings and operations across diverse terrains. With space for a crew of three and up to seven infantrymen, the BMP-2 combines transport capacity with strong battlefield support, making it a reliable and widely used IFV across many armed forces. Estimated unit cost: $0.7–1 million USD
