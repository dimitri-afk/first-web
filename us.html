M1A1-ABRAMS

The M1A1 Abrams is a U.S.-made main battle tank, renowned for its heavy armor, powerful 120mm smoothbore gun, and advanced mobility. While it is not available for civilian sale, surplus units are sometimes transferred to allied militaries; its unit cost was about $10.6 million.

M109-PALADIN

The M109 Paladin is a self-propelled 155mm howitzer that serves as the backbone of U.S. Army artillery support, combining mobility, firepower, and protection in a single platform. Built on a tracked chassis with aluminum armor, it offers defense against small-arms fire and shell fragments while maintaining agility on the battlefield. The Paladin is equipped with a 155mm howitzer capable of firing up to six rounds per minute, supported by a .50 caliber M2 machine gun for close defense. With a crew of six, advanced fire control systems, and a fully traversable turret, it provides precise and sustained indirect fire over ranges exceeding 18 kilometers, depending on ammunition type. Modern variants, such as the M109A6 and M109A7, feature upgraded electronics, improved survivability, and integration with digital battlefield networks, ensuring relevance in contemporary combat operations. Estimated unit cost: $8–10 million USD

M3-BRADLEY

The M3 Bradley Cavalry Fighting Vehicle is a versatile armored platform designed to provide reconnaissance, fire support, and transport for mechanized forces. Built on the proven Bradley chassis, it combines mobility, protection, and firepower in a compact design. The M3 is armed with a 25mm M242 Bushmaster chain gun, a coaxial 7.62mm machine gun, and twin TOW anti-tank missile launchers, giving it the ability to engage both infantry and armored threats effectively. Its aluminum armor, reinforced with steel and composite materials, offers protection against small-arms fire and artillery fragments, while its powerful engine ensures high mobility across diverse terrains. With advanced optics, digital systems, and the capacity to carry a small scout team, the M3 Bradley is a reliable solution for modern reconnaissance and combat missions. Estimated unit cost: $3–4 million USD
